HackManchester 2019 - 24 hours full of coding

Introduction

HackManchester is an annual hackaton organised every year and takes place in the Museum of Science and Industry Manchester. People usually come here as part of a team of 4 and choose a specific challenge from a given list. Afterwards they have 24 hours to build at least a working prototype that would show that their idea can be implemented.Some of them struggle with the complexity of their idea while others simply get their head around and manage to build their fully working product in time. However, one thing is certain, everyone is having a good time(who knows..maybe for the caffeine buzz?).

The Hive and The Bees

My team was composed by my friends from the University of Salford, 3 of us from Computer Science and the designer from Game design and Production. The skills in our team were mixed, some of us were very good at design while others were better at programming.
When it came to choosing a challenge, we thought that the one proposed by the Co-Op would be the most interesting one. The task was "Design a product that would incourage people from your local area to help each other".After some brainstorming and careful consideration our app, "The Hive" was born.
Why Hive and what's up with those bees? Because Manchester of course ! In order to encourage the local popularity of the app we thought that people that help each other are like bees in a hive. All for one and one for all. This was the inspiration for the name of our team as well. We constantly presented ourselves to the judges as being "The bees"

After 24 hours of working...

Wow, after so much work it is finally done. We managed to build fully working Android app in just 24 hours along with a beautiful website to advertise our product.We felt very proud about it and the judges were pleased as well. Very few teams actually manage to go from an idea to an actually working product that can be deployed over multiple devices in just 24 hours.
